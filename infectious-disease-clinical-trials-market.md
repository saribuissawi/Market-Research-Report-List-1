<p><p>Infectious disease clinical trials are categorized into four phases based on development stages. Phase I trials primarily assess safety and dosage in a small group of participants. Phase II trials evaluate efficacy and further safety in a larger cohort. Phase III trials involve extensive testing across diverse populations to confirm effectiveness and monitor side effects. Finally, Phase IV trials occur post-market, collecting additional data on long-term effects and overall effectiveness of the treatment in real-world settings.</p></p>
