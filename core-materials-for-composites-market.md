<p><p>Core materials for composites play a vital role in enhancing the structural integrity and performance of lightweight panels. Foam cores, typically made from materials like PVC or polyurethane, provide excellent insulation and are easy to shape, making them ideal for applications in aerospace and marine industries. Honeycomb cores, constructed from materials like aluminum or paper, offer superior strength-to-weight ratios and stiffness, effectively distributing loads. Both types contribute to reducing overall weight while maintaining durability and performance in various engineering applications.</p></p>
