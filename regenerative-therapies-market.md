<p><p>The regenerative therapies market encompasses various graft options used in tissue repair and regeneration. Allografts involve transplanting tissue from a donor of the same species, enhancing healing without the risk of rejection. Xenografts use tissue from a different species, offering alternative solutions when human tissue is unavailable. Alloplasts are synthetic materials designed to mimic natural tissue, providing a scaffold for regeneration. Together, these options cater to various clinical needs, driving innovation and growth in the regenerative medicine field.</p></p>
