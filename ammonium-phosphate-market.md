<p><p>The ammonium phosphate market primarily consists of two key types: Monoammonium Phosphate (MAP) and Diammonium Phosphate (DAP). MAP, with a higher nitrogen content, is often used in starter fertilizers for acidic soils, promoting early plant growth. DAP, containing more phosphorus, is widely utilized for a broad range of crops and is favored for its high nutrient content and solubility. Both types play crucial roles in agricultural productivity, influencing crop yield and soil fertility across various regions.</p></p>
