Azathioprine and Mycophenolate Mofetil, both immunosuppressants, help maintain long-term remission by inhibiting immune responses. Rituximab, another monoclonal antibody, focuses on depleting B-cells, reducing inflammation. Together, these therapies provide diverse options for managing Devic’s Syndrome, addressing both acute and chronic aspects of the condition.</p></p>
