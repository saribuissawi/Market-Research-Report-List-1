<p><p>Fluorescence In Situ Hybridization (FISH) probes are essential tools in molecular biology that enable the detection and localization of specific nucleic acid sequences within cells or tissue sections. These fluorescently labeled probes bind to complementary DNA or RNA sequences, facilitating the study of genetic abnormalities, chromosomal structural variations, and gene expression patterns. The FISH technique is increasingly utilized in fields such as cancer diagnostics, genetic research, and developmental biology.</p><p>The Fluorescence In Situ Hybridization Probe Market is anticipated to witness significant growth, driven by the rising prevalence of genetic disorders and cancer, alongside advancements in biotechnology and diagnostics.
